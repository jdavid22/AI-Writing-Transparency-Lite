About this badge

AI Writing Transparency

You arrived here from a small note in an email footer, something like ✶ Moderately AI-assisted (~50%) · Learn more. This page explains what that means.

What the badge says

The sender used an AI tool to help write or edit that email, and is telling you so on purpose. The badge gives a rough sense of how much of the wording came from the AI versus the person, and nothing more.

The badge carries none of the email's content: not the original text, not the AI's output, not any chat link. It's just a disclosure, kept deliberately small and clean.

What the effort level means

The percentage is a self-reported, plain-language estimate of how much of the wording is the AI's:

  • Lightly roughly 0 to 33%: the person did most of the writing; AI helped at the edges.
  • Moderately roughly 34 to 66%: a genuine collaboration between person and AI.
  • Heavily roughly 67 to 100%: the AI produced most of the wording, usually from a short prompt.

How the estimate is made

It starts from a simple heuristic. If the sender wrote their own draft and the AI only edited it, the work is credited mostly to the person. If the sender gave a short prompt that the AI expanded into a much longer message, more of the wording is credited to the AI. The sender can adjust the figure by hand, so treat it as an honest gauge, not a precise measurement.

This is a good-faith, self-reported note from the sender. It is not cryptographic proof and not tamper-proof. It reflects what the sender chose to disclose. There's no tracking, no account, and no server: the badge is just a label and a link to this page.
